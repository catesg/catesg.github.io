Communication is one of the most important life skills yet many of us do not get the best out of our interactions communicate, whether speaking, questioning or listening. This can lead to untapped potential and missed opportunities.

Positive Communication helps us understand, deepen and strengthen the ways in which we communicate. It increases the value that communication brings to our sense of self, our relationships, the way we work and the way we live.

Why Positive Communication

Positive communication practices promote well-being within individuals, teams and organisations. By understanding the way we and others communicate and becoming better at doing it, we can strengthen relationships across our lives. We reduce misunderstandings, be clear about what we want, stop wasting time and make our interactions with others truly count.

Schools & Learning Institutions

Student well-being is an important part of developing socially conscious and globally minded individuals ready to take their place in the wider world. Well-being and mental health underpin academic achievement and prepares our young people to lead rich, purposeful lives.

Teams & Organisations

For adult learners and organisations, positive communication and education practices enhance teamwork and productivity. It can attract great candidates, reduce staff turnover and help your organisation stand out as a place people want to do business in, and business with.

Research

Positive Classroom Communication is communication within the classroom that stimulates engagement, creates a constructive learning environment, and promotes student wellbeing. Barriers to PPC arise for a range of reasons; personal communication styles, the environment in which teachers work, or perhaps a general lack of knowledge around communication and the role it plays in the classroom. My current research aims to acquire a 'snapshot' of classroom communication in a private secondary school to determine what barriers exist to PCC, what understandng there is around communication in the classroom, and teachers' perceptions of their interactions.

A lot of communication training seems to be focused on two areas - business communication and relationship counselling. It is not so much for the person on the street, which seems a shame, as we are all communicating, whether we mean to or not! Psychologist and philosopher Paul Watzlawick's first axiom says, "One cannot not communicate." Communication is in everything we do, as much as breathing. It is the glue within, or conduit of, our relationships - personal and professional. Isn't it sensical that to strengthen relationships we should strengthen our communication? Shouldn't we all be more conscious of the myriad of messages we send and receive every day?
